Part 1 Telling Data Stories

The questions below refer to Edward Segel and Jeffrey Heer. Narrative Visualization: Telling Stories with Data.

  • What is the Oxford English Dictionary’s defintion of a narrative?
    The Oxford English Dictionary defines narrative as “an account of a series of events, facts, etc., given in order and with the establishing of connections between them".

  • What is your favorite visualization among the examples in section 3? Explain why in a few words.
    Our favorite vizualisation is “Budget Forecasts, Compared With Reality”. The data complete and illustrate perfectly the text in the left panel. The visualization enriches the text by answering questions such as “HOW?”, “WHEN?” and “WHAT?” when the user mouses-over the chart (hover detaims-on-demand). This visualization is very strucured with a consistent visualization platform, a progress bar, and a “checklist” progress tracker. It is also interative with its animated transitions and interactive slideshow wich don’t take the user to new visual scenes. What we particularly like is that the presentation encourages the user to explore the data and investigate budget forecasts for specific years. There is not too much information like in the visualization “Afghanistan: Behind the Front Line” but gives all the information the user needs to understand the claims and explanations provided in the text.

  • What’s the point of Figure 7?
    The point of figure 7 is to organize the design features into coherent categories (Genre,Visual Narrative, Narrative Strucure) and to relate the 58 visualizations analyzed those specific features. The table highlights for each example its characteristics such as the genre of visual narration, its devices that assist and faciliate narrative, the consistency of its interactive designs, whether it uses storytelling techniques or strategies to engage the user in the interactive functionality, etc.

  • Use Figure 7 to find the most common design choice within each category for the Visual narrative and Narrative structure (the categories within visual narrative are ‘visual structuring’, ‘highlighting’, etc).

    For the Visual narrative, here is the most common design choice within:

    • Visual Structuring: Consistent Visual Platform;
    • Highlighting: Feature Distinction;
    • Transition Guidance: Object Continuity

    For the Narrative Structure, here is the most common design choice within:

    • Ordering: User Directed Path;
    • Interactivity: Filtering / Selection / Search;
    • Messaging: Captions / Headlines

  • Check out Figure 8 and section 4.3.

    • What is your favorite genre of narrative visualization? Why?
      Our favorite genre of narrative visualization is slide show. In fact, it is easy to access the information you need and you can go bak and forward in a easier way than in a video for example which has to be quiet linear. It can contain a lot of other genres of visualization such as flow charts (3.2), annotated graphs or even videos/animations.

    • What is your least favorite genre? Why?
      Our least favorite genre of narrative visualization is video. In fact, they can’t really be interactive and the user can’t explore in its own pace all the explanations provided by the visualization.

Part 2: Visualizing Murders over Time

Exercise: Create a visualization of murders in all of NYC over time.

  • The visualization should be based on a brushable timeline.
  • The timeline should contain the number of murder incident reports each day across the entire time-period.
  • A user should be able to filter the map by selecting a section of the timeline.
  • When the brush is moved or changed the map should update.
  • Optional 1. Create a button that animates a moving brush window.
  • Optional 2. Add a bar-chart that represents the reported murder incidents of each hour in total for all the days selected with the timeline brush.
  • Optional 3. Add the map brush back in and have it cross-filter with the other brush so only the murder incidents filtered by both brushes are shown on the map, barchart and timelineline. Add in the third selection filter on the barchart for good measure.

Please, play with map and timeline brushes
